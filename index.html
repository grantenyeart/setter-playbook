The Setter Playbook

The cold-call framework: open, diagnose, match, and book the Zoom.

GE

Open · Diagnose · Match · Pitch · Book

Read This First

A framework, not a robot script

The lines in here are your defaults. The beats are fixed, the words are yours. You listen, then cater every call to what they actually tell you. No two calls sound the same.

Your one job on this call: book the Zoom with a closer. Not to close, not to pitch everything. Book the call.
Talk track

If you sound like you're reading, you lose. Learn the beats, own the lines, then talk like a normal person who found something they genuinely need.

The Call Map

Every call walks this path

1 Open with the hook
2 Read the fork: got room, or slammed?
3 Diagnose the pain (3 questions)
4 Match the pain to the product
5 Position Rapid Reach, pitch the ONE that fits
6 Handle the brush-offs
7 Book the Zoom
Talk track

The only place it branches is the diagnosis. Nail that and the rest of the call writes itself.

Step 1 · The Opener

Hook them in 10 seconds

Tonality: casual, confident, fast
Lead Gen opener
"Hey, is this [Company]? Perfect, quick question. Could you take on around 10 more details a week right now, or are you all booked up?"
AI Receptionist opener
"Hey, what time do you guys close? ... And if I called past then, would that go straight to voicemail, or do you have something in place that answers it?"
Talk track

Every product has its own opener, all five are laid out in the Dialer Scripts section coming up. The words flex, the beat does not: hook fast, then let their answer open the diagnosis.

Step 1b · Read The Answer

Two roads, both lead to discovery

If YES (they've got room)
"Perfect, that's exactly why I'm calling. I work with detailers getting 15 to 20 extra bookings a week and I'm checking who can handle that volume. I actually found you on Google and your reviews really stood out."
If NO (they're slammed)
"Good for you, sounds like you're busy. Quick question though: are you the one doing most of the details AND answering all the calls?"
Talk track

Never let "I'm busy" end the call. Busy means they're drowning in the work, which is your automation opening. Both answers roll straight into the diagnosis.

Step 2 · Diagnose The Pain

3 questions that tell you what to sell

1
"Where do most of your customers come from right now, Google, word of mouth, Facebook?"
2
"Do you have a website or online booking system set up?"
3
"Are you the one handling most of the calls and messages yourself?"
Talk track

This is the whole game. You can't match a product to a pain you never uncovered. Ask, then listen. Their answers pick the pitch for you.

The Decision Tree

Match the pain to the product

"Word of mouth only, not enough calls"
Lead Gen (rank #1 on Google)
"No website, or an old one that won't convert"
Website
"Doing all the calls himself, missing calls"
AI Automation / Receptionist
"No follow-up, no repeat customers, no-shows"
Detailer Growth System (DGS)
"Wants more high-ticket ceramics"
Facebook ads (ceramic script)
Talk track

Lead with the ONE that matches their biggest pain. Dumping all five is how you overwhelm them and lose the booking.

Step 3 · Position Rapid Reach

Frame it as a growth system

The line
"What we built is a growth system specifically for detailing companies. Some shops we work with do $10k months, some do $30k to $60k. We help detailers scale no matter what stage they're at."
The punch
"Most detailers are amazing at detailing. They just never build the systems behind the business that let it actually grow."
Talk track

You're not a telemarketer, you're the systems expert. This reframes them from "a detailer" to "a business that's missing the engine."

Setter Rule · Pricing Order

Two rules, not one

1
Lead Gen: say it the moment they ask. 10% / ~$20 a lead, I cover ad spend. It sells itself, never stall on it.
2
Everything else: Zoom before the number. Qualify the pain, see if it fits, then let the closer walk them through value before price lands.
3
If they press for AI or DGS pricing: give a range ("starts around $X") and immediately redirect. "They'll walk that through on the call so you know exactly what makes sense for your setup."
Talk track

Lead Gen is the door opener, so price it freely. Everything else is a bigger commitment that needs a closer to explain the value first, or the number just sounds expensive with no context.

The Dialer Scripts

Five scripts, one per product

Same rules, same close, but each product gets its own opener. Dialing one product all day? This is your page: open, ask the one question, pitch, book. Running the full call? Diagnose first, then drop into the matching pitch.

1 Lead Generation
2 AI Receptionist
3 Detailer Growth System
4 Website
5 Facebook / Ceramic
Script 1 · Lead Generation

The offer that opens every door

Opener
"Hey, is this [Company]? Perfect, quick question. Could you take on around 10 more details a week right now, or are you all booked up?"
The pitch, say the price when they ask
"We only ask 10% of your interior-exterior details. So for a $200 detail that's $20 a lead, regardless of the job I send you, even if it turns into a ceramic."
Back it up
"We're bringing in 10 to 15 details a week strictly off getting your company ranked number one on Google, and I cover all the ad spend."
Rule

The one product where you say the price the second they ask. Performance-based, zero upfront risk. State it, back it with proof, book the Zoom.

Script 2 · AI Receptionist

Sell the freedom, not the tech

Opener, then their answer opens the door
"Hey, what time do you guys close? ... Okay, and if I gave you a call past then, would that go straight to voicemail, or do you have something in place that answers it?"
If nothing is in place, pitch it
"That's exactly why I'm calling. If I could automate that fully out, so every call gets booked into your calendar and you never take a phone call again, would that interest you?"
If they hesitate, two options
"We can set it up full, so the AI answers every call and books them in, OR just to catch your missed calls. Either way you never miss a call again."
Rule

Lead with freedom, not tech. The missed-calls option is the easy yes. Let them pick, then book the Zoom. Price: give a range only if pressed, then redirect.

Script 3 · Detailer Growth System

The system behind the business

Opener, then pivot when they say they are booked
"Are you all booked up right now, or could you still take on more this week? ... That's a good problem. So with all those leads and bookings coming in, how are you keeping track of them all, and getting customers to rebook?"
The frame + price
"Instead of just sending leads, we build the whole system so the business can actually scale." Core system starts around $300 to $500 a month.
What it includes
Booking links with upsells, SEO site, lead tracking dashboard, automated reminders, review automation, CRM, and email + text campaigns.
Rule

Our own software, never say white-label. Pitch only the pieces that match the pain they told you about, not the whole list.

Script 4 · Website

For the "bad site or no site" pain

Opener
"Hey, is this [Company]? I was looking online at your company and noticed you guys don't have a website. Is there, like, a place I could book with you, or no?"
The pitch
"A high-converting, SEO website custom to your company. Even after we hand it over, we'll edit it to your liking unlimited times."
Price
About $500 to $750 for the build, then around $18 a month for hosting.
Rule

If they already have a site, ask if people can actually book from their phone. If it won't convert, same pitch.

Script 5 · Facebook / Ceramic

The high-ticket ceramic play

Opener, then their yes opens it
"Hey, do you guys only do car detailing, or do you also do ceramic coatings and stuff like that? ... Awesome. How many more of those are you looking to take on per week?"
The pitch
"Ceramics are a different game, those buyers aren't just searching Google. We run Facebook and Instagram ads built specifically for high-ticket ceramic jobs and route them straight to your calendar."
Rule

Zoom before the number on this one. Book the call, then map the ad budget and setup there.

The Brush-Offs

Handle it, then steer to the Zoom

"How do I know this works?"
"We're already doing this for detailers across the U.S. Higher rankings, consistent bookings, repeat customers."
"What if I don't make money?"
"Lead gen has no upfront risk. We only get paid when we send you qualified leads."
"I need to think about it"
"Totally. Is it the money, the timing, or just wanting to see how it works first?"
"Just send me info"
"Easiest way is a quick Zoom with one of our specialists, they'll walk it visually. Mornings or afternoons?"
Talk track

Every answer ends by pointing back at the Zoom. The objection isn't the enemy, the un-booked call is.

Pre-Close Check · Lead Gen Only

LLC and insurance

The line
"Before we get you onboarded, do you currently have an LLC and insurance set up?"
If no
"No problem, we'll send you the links to get that set up."
If yes
"Perfect."
Talk track

Only Lead Gen needs this, since we're sending them real jobs. For AI, DGS, Website, and Facebook, skip it and go straight to booking the Zoom. Ask it like the deal is already happening.

The Close · Your Only Goal

Book the Zoom with a closer

The line
"I'll get a quick Zoom set up with one of our specialists who'll show you exactly what it looks like and go over everything in a bit more depth. Do you have any availability today?"
1
Push same day first. "Do you have time today?" If no, go to tomorrow.
2
Pin a specific time. "Morning or evening?" then "What about 1pm / 8pm?"
3
Get their email. Send the Zoom link immediately. Give your number: "Text me if anything comes up."
Talk track

Always try for today first, momentum is highest right now. If they push to tomorrow, get the exact time, lock the email, fire the link, and leave your number so they can reschedule instead of ghost.

Lock It In

The ending that makes them show

The line
"Perfect, you're all set. One of our specialists will walk through everything and show you exactly how we set this up for detailing companies. At the end of the call they'll also send you a free full Google optimization video. Great talking to you, speak soon!"
Open with the hook. Dig for the pain. Pitch the one thing that fixes it. Book the Zoom.
