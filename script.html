The Script, Broken Down

The setter playbook gives you the beats. This breaks the actual call down line by line, gives you every objection word for word, and shows you two full calls start to finish. Learn it as a flow, not a read. Then be a person.

01

The Opener

Keep it simple and go straight to the point. Say their name like you already know them, not "is this."

Say this

"Hey, [Company] Detailing? ... Perfect. I was seeing if you could take on about 10 more details a week, or are you fully booked up right now?"

Why the last 6 words matter

If you stop at "10 more details a week" you get hung up on way more. Adding "...or are you fully booked up?" flips it. Now they want to prove themselves to you, and their answer instantly tells you which road the call takes.

If they catch you cold

"You're just cold calling me"

"I am, man. But let me tell you my offer real quick before you hang up on me, it will be worth the 30 seconds."

02

The Fork

The opener sends you down one of two roads. Read the answer, then go.

Road A · "Yeah, I can take more"

"Perfect, good energy! That's actually exactly why I called. I bring detailers 8 to 15 details a week, and I'm just checking who can handle that kind of volume so I can send it their way."

Road B · "No, I'm booked / slammed"

"Two weeks?! My man, you are killing it. What have you done to get booked out that far, you running ads?" (Get excited, get on their team.)

"Booked up" is not a no. It is a different pitch.

Road A means they need work → Lead Gen. Road B means they are drowning in calls and leads → AI Receptionist or the Growth System. Either way, you keep going.

03

Discovery: diagnose like a doctor

A doctor finds the pain before writing a script. Ask a lot of questions, listen, then say the pain back to them with a dollar figure on it so they feel it.

The question ladder

Ask, in order, based on where they lead you

"Where do you get most of your jobs, word of mouth, Google, Facebook?"

"Are you running any marketing right now, or have you before? What on?"

"Are you the one answering the calls AND doing the detailing?"

"How many calls a day you think you're getting?"

"What time do you close? If I called after hours, straight to voicemail, or do you have something in place?"

"Roughly how many calls you missing a week? And what's your average interior-exterior detail run?"

Then clarify the pain (out loud, with the number)

The math that closes

Detailing shops miss about 1 in 3 calls. So: "You said 30 calls a week, that's 10 going missing. At a $250 ticket, that's $2,500 a week walking out the door, every week." Say it loud. Now they feel it, and you have earned the right to offer the fix.

04

The Pitches

Pitch the ONE product that matches the pain you just diagnosed. Never dump all five.

Lead Gen (Google Ads) · the door-opener

The pitch

"We get your company ranked number one on Google, so anytime someone searches 'detailing near me,' you own that spot. That brings you 8 to 15 details a week. No money up front, fully performance based, and we cover the ad spend."

The price (pattern interrupt)

"How much? That's the best part, you ready? No money up front. We only charge 10% of your interior-exterior detail, regardless of the job I bring you. So Dexter, if your detail is $250, I'm only asking $25. And even if I send you a $1,000 ceramic coating, still just $25."

Note

This is the ONLY product where you say the price on the phone, it sells itself. It is almost a no-brainer. For a defensive, "I get these calls all the time" client, go straight here.

AI Receptionist · sell the freedom

The pitch (after the missed-call math)

"If I told you I had something that answers every one of those missed calls so they never go to another detailer, they stay with you, would you at least be open to hearing what it sounds like? Two ways we set it up: it can be your full 24/7 receptionist, or just a backup that catches the calls you miss. Either way, you never lose a job to a missed call again."

Note

Modeled in their own voice, hyper-realistic. The backup (missed-calls-only) is the easy yes almost nobody turns down. Sell the freedom of never touching the phone, not the tech.

Website

The opener (when they have no site)

"Hey, I was actually looking online to book with you guys and couldn't find a website. Do you have a place people can book, or no?"

The pitch

"We build two kinds. One that just looks clean, and one that's SEO and AEO, meaning you pop up on Google AND on ChatGPT when people ask for a detailer. I can't quote you a price blind, same as you can't price a detail without seeing the car. Let's hop on a quick Zoom, I'll show you a demo."

Detailer Growth System (DGS)

The entry

"All these jobs and customers coming in, are you tracking them in a CRM, or are they kind of just sitting up in your head?"

The pitch

"Our Growth System tracks every single lead, sends mass texts to re-activate old customers with one click, auto-requests a Google review after every detail, and can add a booking page to your site. Here's the big one: most shops slow down in winter because they never kept track of old clients. We work with a guy in Minnesota who used to quit detailing half the year for a 9-to-5. First year on our system, he detailed year-round. Never went back."

The campaign math

"You do 10 details a week, that's 40 a month. Imagine you send a ceramic-coating campaign to those 40 people. Convert 10%, that's 4 ceramic jobs you just pulled out of customers you already had."

Meta / Facebook ads (ceramic)

The qualifier + pitch

"Do you only do detailing, or ceramic coatings too? ... How many more ceramic jobs could you take on a week? ... Perfect. We run Facebook and Instagram ads that bring in 3 to 4 high-ticket ceramic jobs a week. Let's hop on a Zoom to see if it's a fit."

Note

Pricing lives on the closing call. You just qualify the interest and book.

05

Book the Zoom

This is the only thing you're actually here to do. Frame it as no-pressure and flip the ego.

Say this

"Let's hop on a quick Zoom with our closer so you can see exactly how it works and we can see if you'd even be a good fit for us. I'm not asking for your card or anything today, worst case, we make a friend."

Then lock it, never yes/no: "Today or tomorrow?" → "Morning or evening?" → "What about 2pm?" → get their last name + best email → book it on the Rapid Reach page.
The ego flip

"See if you're a good fit for us" puts the pressure on them, not you. You just handed them a crazy opportunity. If they mention a business partner: "Perfect, let's get all three of us on so everyone's on the same page."

06

Objection Handling

An objection is not a wall, it's a request for more certainty. Handle it, then point right back at the Zoom every single time.

What's this going to cost me?
Say this (any product except Lead Gen)

"Honestly, it's like you calling me to detail your car, I can't price it until I see it. Might have pet hair, might be spotless. It depends what you're looking to implement. That's exactly what the Zoom is for, we'll build it to your shop. Let's hop on."

Why

Don't give a random number or even a range, it only sounds expensive with no context. For Lead Gen, do the opposite: hit them with the pattern interrupt price, it's your strongest card.

I need to think about it
Say this

"Totally fair. Usually when someone says that, it's one of two things, money or trust. Real quick, do you think this would actually solve the problem we just talked about? ... Then let's at least hop on the call and see if it's a fit, no pressure."

If they've been "thinking about it" forever

"You told me you've wanted this for two years. Be honest, what changes from thinking about it today versus tomorrow? You've got the chance to fix it right now. Are you the owner who steps up and makes a decision, or the one who stays in the same cycle? Who do you want to be today?"

I get these calls all the time / you're the same as everyone
Say this

"I hear that all the time, until I tell people my offer. No money up front, only 10% of your detail, regardless of the job. I bet you get a lot of these calls, but I promise you have never heard a model like this one. Give me five seconds."

Why

Defensive client = go straight to Lead Gen. "No money up front" disarms the wall instantly.

Is this one of those AI things? / AI is garbage, my customers like a real person
Say this

"That's exactly what I said before I got into this, and what every one of our customers said before they heard it. We model it in your own voice, it sounds hyper-realistic. And we can set it to only catch your missed calls, so the ones you answer still get the real you. You've got to hear it, that's what everyone says right up until they do. Let me show you on the Zoom."

Just send me some info
Say this

"I could, but info in an email doesn't show you much. Easiest is a quick 15-minute Zoom where we walk it visually. Worst case, we make a friend. Today or tomorrow better?"

How do I know this even works?
Say this

"We're already doing it for detailers all over the US, consistent bookings and repeat customers. And with the Google Ads, we literally show you what we do for two weeks first. That's how you know."

What if I don't make any money?
Say this

"That's the beauty of the lead gen, there's no upfront risk. We only get paid when we actually send you qualified jobs. And we show you before you commit."

I need to talk to my business partner
Say this

"Perfect, let's get all three of us on the Zoom so you're both on the same page and nobody has to relay it. What works for the two of you, today or tomorrow?"

Every objection ends the same way: back to the Zoom.

09

Call Teardown

A real first-week call from one of our own setters, Dexter. A booked, skeptical 30-year shop that tried to end the call twice, and he still booked the Zoom. This is the good kind of hard. Watch what he nails, then what to clean up, green flags and red flags on the same real call.

The recording · ~6 min
What he did right
  • Didn't fold on "no, we're booked." Hit "you must be killing it" and kept the call alive instead of hanging up.
  • Refused the brush-off. When the owner said "hit me up in the off-season," he came back with "only reason I ask..." and reopened it.
  • Pivoted to the right product. Booked shop → after-hours calls → AI receptionist. He matched the pain.
  • Ran the money math out loud off their real ticket ($325 a detail).
  • Adapted on the fly. They already had an answering service, so he sold after-hours coverage + booking straight into the calendar + follow-up.
  • Ground through a messy scheduling mess (vacation, time zones) and still locked a specific slot.
  • Closed on a this-or-that, not a yes/no, and got the name and email.
What to clean up
  • Diagnose before you math. He put dollars on missed calls before confirming they miss any, and got "we answer every call." Ask first, then math their number.
  • Nail the name. He mixed it up more than once. Grab it early and use the real one all call.
  • Be clear on times. "12:12am" cost a minute of confusion. Say "5pm Eastern," then repeat the booking back clean.
  • Tighten the opener. Straight to "you guys booked up, or could you take 10 more details a week?"
  • Load the AI reveal. When they ask "is this AI?": "It is, modeled in your voice. That's what everyone says right before they hear it."
  • Frame the Zoom as the closer's call, not "I look forward to working with you."
